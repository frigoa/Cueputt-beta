Stroke Metrics

Four numbers.
All that matters.

Every metric maps to what coaches teach and research supports.

↩
Backswing Time

How long your putter takes to reach the top. Consistency here is the foundation of repeatable tempo.

Default goal: 0.6s · Crown-adjustable
↪
Forward Swing Time

Time from transition to contact. Your back-to-forward ratio determines the feel and rhythm of your stroke.

Default goal: 0.3s · 2:1 ratio
⏱
Tempo Ratio

The ratio of backswing to forward swing. Tour pros cluster tightly around 2:1. CuePutt shows yours after every putt.

Tour pro range: 1.9:1 – 2.1:1
🎯
Impact Acceleration

What the putter is doing at contact. Elite putters are often decelerating at impact — what matters is that your acceleration profile is consistent stroke to stroke.

Wrist or mounted. Clip your Watch to the putter grip — better data, different view. Beta testers are helping us find out which works best.

FAQ

Good questions.

Research consistently shows that putting performance is tightly tied to stroke rhythm. Tour professionals cluster around 68–84 BPM and maintain a 2:1 backswing-to-forward-swing ratio. Training with a metronome ingrains this rhythm so it becomes automatic under pressure. The gap has always been knowing whether the practice is actually working. CuePutt fills that gap.
Tempo ratio is the relationship between your backswing duration and your forward swing. A 2:1 ratio means your backswing takes twice as long as your forward swing — the most common ratio among elite putters. Too high and you're rushing the forward stroke. Too low and you're decelerating. CuePutt shows your ratio after every putt.
Impact acceleration measures what the putter head is doing at the moment of contact. Decelerating into impact is the most common fault in amateur putting — it causes a closing face, poor contact, and inconsistent distance control. The goal is a stroke that stays committed through the ball. CuePutt measures this after every putt.
No. The metronome and all four stroke metrics run entirely on your Apple Watch. Leave your phone in your bag. The only feature requiring a nearby iPhone is full Buddy AI coaching — that's a future feature. Everything in the current beta is fully standalone.
Metronome and stroke measurement work on any Apple Watch running watchOS 10 or later. On-device Buddy reporting requires Series 9 or later. Full Buddy AI coaching (coming later) will require a paired Apple Intelligence-capable iPhone (15 Pro or later).
Wrist mode is standard. Mounted mode clips your Watch to the grip end of your putter, giving sensors a different reference frame that may improve accuracy. Beta testers are helping us answer two questions: are the metrics more reliable when mounted? And do you like seeing the screen while you putt?
